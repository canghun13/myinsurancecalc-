NFIP vs. Private Flood Insurance

Most flood insurance in the U.S. is provided through FEMA's National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP), but private flood insurance options have expanded significantly and may offer lower premiums, higher coverage limits, or better terms for some properties.

NFIP Private
Max building coverage $250,000 Varies (often higher)
Max contents $100,000 Varies
Waiting period 30 days Often 10–14 days
Cancellation risk Low Higher in high-risk areas
Loss of use coverage No Often included

Why Standard Homeowners Insurance Doesn't Cover Floods

Standard HO-3 homeowners policies specifically exclude flood damage regardless of cause — including heavy rain, storm surge, river overflow, and groundwater seepage. Flood insurance is a completely separate policy. If you've ever had water enter your home from outside, that may qualify as a flood claim — but only if you had a separate flood policy in force.
