Why Is EV Insurance More Expensive?

Electric vehicles cost significantly more to insure than comparable gas-powered cars — often 15–40% more. Understanding why can help you find ways to reduce your premium.

Higher Repair Costs

EV repairs require specialized technicians and parts. The battery pack alone can cost $10,000–$20,000+ to replace. Even minor fender-benders can be expensive because EVs use aluminum frames and specialized electronics that are costly to repair.

Battery Replacement Risk

Insurers factor in the risk of battery damage in accidents. A damaged battery may need full replacement, dramatically increasing claim costs compared to a standard engine repair.

Higher Vehicle Values

Most EVs cost more than equivalent gas vehicles, which means higher comprehensive and collision premiums. As EV prices come down — particularly for models like the Chevy Equinox EV — this gap is narrowing.

How to Reduce Your EV Insurance Premium

Shop multiple insurers — some specialize in EVs and offer better rates. Raise your deductible to lower your monthly premium. Ask about EV-specific discounts. Consider telematics programs that reward safe driving. Bundle your home and auto insurance for a multi-policy discount.
