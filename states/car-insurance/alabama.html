Minimum Coverage Requirements

Alabama requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of 25/50/25 — meaning $25,000 per person for bodily injury, $50,000 per accident for bodily injury, and $25,000 for property damage. PIP coverage is not required in Alabama, though it may be available as an optional add-on. These are the legal minimums; most insurance professionals recommend carrying significantly higher limits to protect personal assets in a serious accident. A common recommendation is at least 100/300/100, which provides substantially more protection without a proportionally large premium increase.

Average Car Insurance Costs in Alabama

Drivers in Alabama typically pay approximately $1,480 per year ($123/month) for car insurance, based on 2026 industry average data. Individual premiums vary significantly based on driving record, age, vehicle type, annual mileage, and selected coverage levels. Drivers with clean records may see rates well below the state average, while those with violations, claims, or high-risk profiles may pay substantially more.

State Regulations and Key Rules

Alabama is an at-fault state. Drivers are not required to carry uninsured motorist coverage, though it is strongly recommended given the high uninsured driver rate. SR-22 filings are required after certain violations.

What Makes Alabama Unique

Alabama has one of the higher rates of uninsured drivers in the U.S., making uninsured motorist coverage especially valuable even though it is not mandatory.

Uninsured Drivers in Alabama

Approximately 19.5% of drivers in Alabama are estimated to be uninsured. Carrying uninsured and underinsured motorist coverage provides important protection against the financial impact of an accident with an uninsured or underinsured driver. Uninsured motorist (UM) coverage pays for your medical expenses and vehicle damage if you are hit by a driver without insurance or with insufficient coverage. Underinsured motorist (UIM) coverage fills the gap when the at-fault driver's policy limits are too low to cover your losses.

Risk Factors Specific to Alabama

Drivers in southern Alabama, particularly coastal areas, should consider comprehensive coverage for hurricane and flooding risk. Deer collisions are common in rural northern Alabama in fall months.

How to Save on Car Insurance in Alabama

Alabama insurers heavily weigh credit scores, so improving your credit can meaningfully lower your premium. Bundling auto with home insurance typically saves 10–15% with most major carriers operating in the state. Completing a state-approved defensive driving course can also qualify you for a discount.

  • Compare quotes from at least 3–5 insurers annually — rates for identical coverage can vary by hundreds of dollars.
  • State minimums are typically insufficient for serious accidents — consider liability limits of at least 100/300/100.
  • Ask about bundling discounts if you also need home or renters insurance.
  • Consider telematics or usage-based insurance if you drive fewer miles than average or primarily drive during off-peak hours.
  • Review your coverage annually, especially after major life changes: moving, buying a new vehicle, or adding a teen driver.
