Minimum Coverage Requirements

Utah requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of 25/65/15 — meaning $25,000 per person for bodily injury, $65,000 per accident for bodily injury, and $15,000 for property damage. Personal Injury Protection (PIP) is also required, covering medical expenses regardless of fault. These are the legal minimums; most insurance professionals recommend carrying significantly higher limits to protect personal assets in a serious accident. A common recommendation is at least 100/300/100, which provides substantially more protection without a proportionally large premium increase.

Average Car Insurance Costs in Utah

Drivers in Utah typically pay approximately $1,340 per year ($112/month) for car insurance, based on 2026 industry average data. Individual premiums vary significantly based on driving record, age, vehicle type, annual mileage, and selected coverage levels. Drivers with clean records may see rates well below the state average, while those with violations, claims, or high-risk profiles may pay substantially more.

State Regulations and Key Rules

Utah is a no-fault state requiring PIP of $3,000. Utah has an unusual split limit with $65,000 per accident for bodily injury, higher than most states. Uninsured motorist coverage must be offered.

What Makes Utah Unique

Utah's $65,000 per-accident bodily injury limit is one of the highest standard minimums in the country, offering better protection per accident than the more common $50,000 limit seen in states with 25/50 requirements.

Uninsured Drivers in Utah

Approximately 8.2% of drivers in Utah are estimated to be uninsured. Carrying uninsured and underinsured motorist coverage provides important protection against the financial impact of an accident with an uninsured or underinsured driver. Uninsured motorist (UM) coverage pays for your medical expenses and vehicle damage if you are hit by a driver without insurance or with insufficient coverage. Underinsured motorist (UIM) coverage fills the gap when the at-fault driver's policy limits are too low to cover your losses.

Risk Factors Specific to Utah

Utah's mountain roads and canyon highways present serious winter driving hazards — Provo Canyon and I-80 mountain sections are particularly dangerous in winter. Salt Lake Valley experiences temperature inversions that trap pollutants and reduce visibility. Southern Utah's desert roads see flash flooding during monsoon season.

How to Save on Car Insurance in Utah

Salt Lake City and surrounding Wasatch Front cities have higher rates than rural Utah — drivers in St. George and southern Utah benefit from lower premiums. Utah's growing population and increasing traffic in the Salt Lake Valley have pushed urban rates higher in recent years. Good driver discounts are competitive among major carriers.

  • Compare quotes from at least 3–5 insurers annually — rates for identical coverage can vary by hundreds of dollars.
  • Utah's minimum liability requirement of 25/65/15 is the legal floor, not a safe target — since Utah already requires PIP coverage, review whether that limit alone would cover a serious injury claim. Most agents recommend at least 100/300/100 for meaningful asset protection.
  • Ask about bundling discounts if you also need home or renters insurance.
  • With an estimated 8.2% of Utah drivers uninsured, carrying uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage above the state minimum is one of the highest-value add-ons you can buy.
  • Review your coverage annually, especially after major life changes: moving, buying a new vehicle, or adding a teen driver.
