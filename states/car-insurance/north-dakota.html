Minimum Coverage Requirements

North Dakota requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of 25/50/25 — meaning $25,000 per person for bodily injury, $50,000 per accident for bodily injury, and $25,000 for property damage. Personal Injury Protection (PIP) is also required, covering medical expenses regardless of fault. These are the legal minimums; most insurance professionals recommend carrying significantly higher limits to protect personal assets in a serious accident. A common recommendation is at least 100/300/100, which provides substantially more protection without a proportionally large premium increase.

Average Car Insurance Costs in North Dakota

Drivers in North Dakota typically pay approximately $1,120 per year ($93/month) for car insurance, based on 2026 industry average data. Individual premiums vary significantly based on driving record, age, vehicle type, annual mileage, and selected coverage levels. Drivers with clean records may see rates well below the state average, while those with violations, claims, or high-risk profiles may pay substantially more.

State Regulations and Key Rules

North Dakota is a no-fault state requiring PIP coverage. Uninsured and underinsured motorist coverage is also required. The state's harsh winters and rural roads contribute to certain claim types, though overall rates remain moderate.

What Makes North Dakota Unique

North Dakota requires both PIP and UM/UIM coverage, providing drivers with comprehensive baseline protection. Severe winter weather and wildlife collisions are the most common claim drivers.

Uninsured Drivers in North Dakota

Approximately 7.4% of drivers in North Dakota are estimated to be uninsured. This is among the lower uninsured rates in the country, though carrying uninsured motorist coverage remains a sound financial decision. Uninsured motorist (UM) coverage pays for your medical expenses and vehicle damage if you are hit by a driver without insurance or with insufficient coverage. Underinsured motorist (UIM) coverage fills the gap when the at-fault driver's policy limits are too low to cover your losses.

Risk Factors Specific to North Dakota

North Dakota experiences some of the most severe winter driving conditions in the nation — blizzards, ice, and sub-zero temperatures make winter driving hazardous from October through April. Deer and antelope collisions are common on rural highways. Spring flooding along the Red River periodically affects roads in the eastern part of the state.

How to Save on Car Insurance in North Dakota

North Dakota's rates are among the lower in the upper Midwest. Multi-policy bundling with farm policies is common and effective for rural drivers. Winter tires can sometimes qualify for discounts with certain carriers.

  • Compare quotes from at least 3–5 insurers annually — rates for identical coverage can vary by hundreds of dollars.
  • North Dakota's minimum liability requirement of 25/50/25 is the legal floor, not a safe target — since North Dakota already requires PIP coverage, review whether that limit alone would cover a serious injury claim. Most agents recommend at least 100/300/100 for meaningful asset protection.
  • Ask about bundling discounts if you also need home or renters insurance.
  • With an estimated 7.4% of North Dakota drivers uninsured, carrying uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage above the state minimum is one of the highest-value add-ons you can buy.
  • Review your coverage annually, especially after major life changes: moving, buying a new vehicle, or adding a teen driver.
