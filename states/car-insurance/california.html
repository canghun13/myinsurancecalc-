Minimum Coverage Requirements

California requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of 15/30/5 — meaning $15,000 per person for bodily injury, $30,000 per accident for bodily injury, and $5,000 for property damage. PIP coverage is not required in California, though it may be available as an optional add-on. These are the legal minimums; most insurance professionals recommend carrying significantly higher limits to protect personal assets in a serious accident. A common recommendation is at least 100/300/100, which provides substantially more protection without a proportionally large premium increase.

Average Car Insurance Costs in California

Drivers in California typically pay approximately $1,810 per year ($151/month) for car insurance, based on 2026 industry average data. Individual premiums vary significantly based on driving record, age, vehicle type, annual mileage, and selected coverage levels. Drivers with clean records may see rates well below the state average, while those with violations, claims, or high-risk profiles may pay substantially more.

State Regulations and Key Rules

California has some of the lowest minimum liability limits in the country. As of 2025, new minimums are phased in at 30/60/15. It is an at-fault state. Insurers cannot use credit scores to set rates in California.

What Makes California Unique

California prohibits the use of credit history in determining car insurance premiums, which is unique among most U.S. states. Rates are instead based on driving record, miles driven, and years of experience.

Uninsured Drivers in California

Approximately 16.6% of drivers in California are estimated to be uninsured. Carrying uninsured and underinsured motorist coverage provides important protection against the financial impact of an accident with an uninsured or underinsured driver. Uninsured motorist (UM) coverage pays for your medical expenses and vehicle damage if you are hit by a driver without insurance or with insufficient coverage. Underinsured motorist (UIM) coverage fills the gap when the at-fault driver's policy limits are too low to cover your losses.

Risk Factors Specific to California

Wildfire smoke and evacuation routes can affect driving conditions significantly in fire-prone areas. Earthquake damage to roads increases accident risk. Los Angeles and Bay Area traffic congestion drives higher-than-average accident frequency and repair costs.

How to Save on Car Insurance in California

Because California bans credit-based pricing, your driving record has an outsized impact on your rate — keeping a clean record is the single most effective way to lower premiums. Mileage-based insurance programs are popular and cost-effective for low-mileage urban drivers. Good driver discounts (no violations in 3 years) are substantial in California.

  • Compare quotes from at least 3–5 insurers annually — rates for identical coverage can vary by hundreds of dollars.
  • State minimums are typically insufficient for serious accidents — consider liability limits of at least 100/300/100.
  • Ask about bundling discounts if you also need home or renters insurance.
  • Consider telematics or usage-based insurance if you drive fewer miles than average or primarily drive during off-peak hours.
  • Review your coverage annually, especially after major life changes: moving, buying a new vehicle, or adding a teen driver.
