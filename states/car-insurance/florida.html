Minimum Coverage Requirements

Florida requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of 10/20/10 — meaning $10,000 per person for bodily injury, $20,000 per accident for bodily injury, and $10,000 for property damage. Personal Injury Protection (PIP) is also required, covering medical expenses regardless of fault. These are the legal minimums; most insurance professionals recommend carrying significantly higher limits to protect personal assets in a serious accident. A common recommendation is at least 100/300/100, which provides substantially more protection without a proportionally large premium increase.

Average Car Insurance Costs in Florida

Drivers in Florida typically pay approximately $2,560 per year ($213/month) for car insurance, based on 2026 industry average data. Individual premiums vary significantly based on driving record, age, vehicle type, annual mileage, and selected coverage levels. Drivers with clean records may see rates well below the state average, while those with violations, claims, or high-risk profiles may pay substantially more.

State Regulations and Key Rules

Florida is a no-fault state requiring $10,000 in PIP and $10,000 in property damage liability. Bodily injury liability is not required for most drivers, though it is strongly recommended. Florida has one of the highest uninsured driver rates in the nation.

What Makes Florida Unique

Florida's unusual minimum requirements — no mandatory bodily injury liability — mean at-fault drivers can legally cause serious injuries without bodily injury coverage. Drivers should strongly consider carrying bodily injury liability well above the state minimum.

Uninsured Drivers in Florida

Approximately 20.4% of drivers in Florida are estimated to be uninsured. This is a notably high uninsured rate — UM/UIM coverage is strongly recommended and, in many cases, essential for adequate financial protection. Uninsured motorist (UM) coverage pays for your medical expenses and vehicle damage if you are hit by a driver without insurance or with insufficient coverage. Underinsured motorist (UIM) coverage fills the gap when the at-fault driver's policy limits are too low to cover your losses.

Risk Factors Specific to Florida

Hurricane season (June–November) significantly increases comprehensive claims statewide. Miami and Fort Lauderdale have extremely high rates of insurance fraud, which drives up costs for all drivers. Flooding risk is significant across much of South Florida.

How to Save on Car Insurance in Florida

Florida rates vary enormously by ZIP code — Miami-Dade rates can be nearly double those in the Panhandle. Shopping quotes from multiple insurers is especially important in Florida. Telematics and pay-per-mile programs can offer significant savings for drivers who avoid rush hour or drive limited miles. Installing anti-theft devices helps in high-theft urban areas.

  • Compare quotes from at least 3–5 insurers annually — rates for identical coverage can vary by hundreds of dollars.
  • Florida's minimum liability requirement of 10/20/10 is the legal floor, not a safe target — since Florida already requires PIP coverage, review whether that limit alone would cover a serious injury claim. Most agents recommend at least 100/300/100 for meaningful asset protection.
  • Ask about bundling discounts if you also need home or renters insurance.
  • With an estimated 20.4% of Florida drivers uninsured, carrying uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage above the state minimum is one of the highest-value add-ons you can buy.
  • Review your coverage annually, especially after major life changes: moving, buying a new vehicle, or adding a teen driver.
