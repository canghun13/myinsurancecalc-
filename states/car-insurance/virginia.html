Minimum Coverage Requirements

Virginia requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of 30/60/20 — meaning $30,000 per person for bodily injury, $60,000 per accident for bodily injury, and $20,000 for property damage. PIP coverage is not required in Virginia, though it may be available as an optional add-on. These are the legal minimums; most insurance professionals recommend carrying significantly higher limits to protect personal assets in a serious accident. A common recommendation is at least 100/300/100, which provides substantially more protection without a proportionally large premium increase.

Average Car Insurance Costs in Virginia

Drivers in Virginia typically pay approximately $1,290 per year ($108/month) for car insurance, based on 2026 industry average data. Individual premiums vary significantly based on driving record, age, vehicle type, annual mileage, and selected coverage levels. Drivers with clean records may see rates well below the state average, while those with violations, claims, or high-risk profiles may pay substantially more.

State Regulations and Key Rules

Virginia eliminated its uninsured motorist fee option in 2024, making car insurance mandatory for all drivers. Uninsured motorist coverage is required. Virginia uses a contributory negligence rule.

What Makes Virginia Unique

Virginia made car insurance fully mandatory as of 2024, closing the previous loophole that allowed drivers to pay a $500 uninsured motorist fee instead of carrying insurance. The contributory negligence rule also means any fault can bar recovery.

Uninsured Drivers in Virginia

Approximately 10.8% of drivers in Virginia are estimated to be uninsured. Carrying uninsured and underinsured motorist coverage provides important protection against the financial impact of an accident with an uninsured or underinsured driver. Uninsured motorist (UM) coverage pays for your medical expenses and vehicle damage if you are hit by a driver without insurance or with insufficient coverage. Underinsured motorist (UIM) coverage fills the gap when the at-fault driver's policy limits are too low to cover your losses.

Risk Factors Specific to Virginia

Hampton Roads area (Norfolk, Virginia Beach, Chesapeake) faces significant flooding risk from nor'easters and tropical storms. Northern Virginia I-66 and I-95 corridors have high accident frequency during rush hours. Virginia's western mountains see heavy snow and ice in winter.

How to Save on Car Insurance in Virginia

Northern Virginia (Arlington and Alexandria) rates are above the state average due to DC-area traffic and theft. Virginia's contributory negligence rule makes dashcams a worthwhile investment. Military personnel in Virginia often qualify for special discounts through USAA or other military-affiliated insurers.

  • Compare quotes from at least 3–5 insurers annually — rates for identical coverage can vary by hundreds of dollars.
  • Virginia's minimum liability requirement of 30/60/20 is the legal floor, not a safe target — since PIP isn't required in Virginia, a separate medical payments (MedPay) add-on is worth pricing out. Most agents recommend at least 100/300/100 for meaningful asset protection.
  • Ask about bundling discounts if you also need home or renters insurance.
  • With an estimated 10.8% of Virginia drivers uninsured, carrying uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage above the state minimum is one of the highest-value add-ons you can buy.
  • Review your coverage annually, especially after major life changes: moving, buying a new vehicle, or adding a teen driver.
