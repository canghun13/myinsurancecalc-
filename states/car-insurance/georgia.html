Minimum Coverage Requirements

Georgia requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of 25/50/25 — meaning $25,000 per person for bodily injury, $50,000 per accident for bodily injury, and $25,000 for property damage.. PIP coverage is not required, though it may be available as an optional add-on. These are the legal minimums; most insurance professionals recommend carrying significantly higher limits to protect personal assets in a serious accident.

Average Car Insurance Costs in Georgia

Drivers in Georgia typically pay approximately $1,750 per year ($146/month) for car insurance, based on industry average data for 2026. Individual premiums vary based on driving record, age, vehicle type, credit history (where permitted), annual mileage, and selected coverage levels. Drivers with clean records and good credit may see rates well below this average, while those with violations or claims may pay significantly more.

State Regulations and Key Rules

Georgia is an at-fault state. Uninsured motorist coverage is required unless specifically rejected in writing. Georgia uses a modified comparative fault rule, meaning drivers less than 50% at fault can recover damages.

What Makes Georgia Unique

Georgia's comparative fault system means that even partially at-fault drivers may recover compensation, making adequate liability coverage important on both sides of an accident.

Uninsured Drivers in Georgia

Approximately 12.4% of drivers in Georgia are estimated to be uninsured. Carrying uninsured motorist coverage provides important protection against the financial impact of an accident with an uninsured driver. Uninsured motorist coverage pays for your medical bills and vehicle damage if you are hit by a driver who has no insurance or insufficient coverage.

Tips for Georgia Drivers

  • State minimums are typically insufficient for serious accidents — consider liability limits of at least 100/300/100 for better protection.
  • Compare quotes from multiple insurers; rates in Georgia can vary significantly between providers for the same coverage.
  • Ask about available discounts: bundling home and auto, good driver, good student, low mileage, and defensive driving discounts are commonly available.
  • Review your coverage annually, especially after major life changes such as moving, buying a new vehicle, or adding a teen driver.
