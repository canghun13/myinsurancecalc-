Minimum Coverage Requirements

Mississippi requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of 25/50/25 — meaning $25,000 per person for bodily injury, $50,000 per accident for bodily injury, and $25,000 for property damage. PIP coverage is not required in Mississippi, though it may be available as an optional add-on. These are the legal minimums; most insurance professionals recommend carrying significantly higher limits to protect personal assets in a serious accident. A common recommendation is at least 100/300/100, which provides substantially more protection without a proportionally large premium increase.

Average Car Insurance Costs in Mississippi

Drivers in Mississippi typically pay approximately $1,500 per year ($125/month) for car insurance, based on 2026 industry average data. Individual premiums vary significantly based on driving record, age, vehicle type, annual mileage, and selected coverage levels. Drivers with clean records may see rates well below the state average, while those with violations, claims, or high-risk profiles may pay substantially more.

State Regulations and Key Rules

Mississippi has the highest uninsured driver rate in the United States. It is an at-fault state, and uninsured motorist coverage, while not required, is critical given the extremely high rate of uninsured drivers.

What Makes Mississippi Unique

With nearly 30% of drivers uninsured, Mississippi presents significant risk for drivers who do not carry uninsured motorist coverage. Experts strongly recommend adding this coverage despite it not being legally required.

Uninsured Drivers in Mississippi

Approximately 29.4% of drivers in Mississippi are estimated to be uninsured. This is a notably high uninsured rate — UM/UIM coverage is strongly recommended and, in many cases, essential for adequate financial protection. Uninsured motorist (UM) coverage pays for your medical expenses and vehicle damage if you are hit by a driver without insurance or with insufficient coverage. Underinsured motorist (UIM) coverage fills the gap when the at-fault driver's policy limits are too low to cover your losses.

Risk Factors Specific to Mississippi

Gulf Coast Mississippi faces significant hurricane risk, making comprehensive coverage essential for coastal residents. Jackson has elevated vehicle theft rates. Tornado risk is significant across central and northern Mississippi.

How to Save on Car Insurance in Mississippi

Uninsured motorist coverage is non-negotiable in Mississippi given the nearly 30% uninsured rate — the premium cost is modest relative to the risk. Jackson has higher rates than coastal or northern cities due to theft and accident frequency. Bundling home and auto with regional carriers can yield meaningful savings.

  • Compare quotes from at least 3–5 insurers annually — rates for identical coverage can vary by hundreds of dollars.
  • Mississippi's minimum liability requirement of 25/50/25 is the legal floor, not a safe target — since PIP isn't required in Mississippi, a separate medical payments (MedPay) add-on is worth pricing out. Most agents recommend at least 100/300/100 for meaningful asset protection.
  • Ask about bundling discounts if you also need home or renters insurance.
  • With an estimated 29.4% of Mississippi drivers uninsured, carrying uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage above the state minimum is one of the highest-value add-ons you can buy.
  • Review your coverage annually, especially after major life changes: moving, buying a new vehicle, or adding a teen driver.
