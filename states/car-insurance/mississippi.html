Minimum Coverage Requirements

Mississippi requires drivers to carry minimum liability insurance of 25/50/25 — meaning $25,000 per person for bodily injury, $50,000 per accident for bodily injury, and $25,000 for property damage.. PIP coverage is not required, though it may be available as an optional add-on. These are the legal minimums; most insurance professionals recommend carrying significantly higher limits to protect personal assets in a serious accident.

Average Car Insurance Costs in Mississippi

Drivers in Mississippi typically pay approximately $1,500 per year ($125/month) for car insurance, based on industry average data for 2026. Individual premiums vary based on driving record, age, vehicle type, credit history (where permitted), annual mileage, and selected coverage levels. Drivers with clean records and good credit may see rates well below this average, while those with violations or claims may pay significantly more.

State Regulations and Key Rules

Mississippi has the highest uninsured driver rate in the United States. It is an at-fault state, and uninsured motorist coverage, while not required, is critical given the extremely high rate of uninsured drivers.

What Makes Mississippi Unique

With nearly 30% of drivers uninsured, Mississippi presents significant risk for drivers who do not carry uninsured motorist coverage. Experts strongly recommend adding this coverage despite it not being legally required.

Uninsured Drivers in Mississippi

Approximately 29.4% of drivers in Mississippi are estimated to be uninsured. This is a notably high rate, making uninsured motorist coverage especially important for drivers in this state. Uninsured motorist coverage pays for your medical bills and vehicle damage if you are hit by a driver who has no insurance or insufficient coverage.

Tips for Mississippi Drivers

  • State minimums are typically insufficient for serious accidents — consider liability limits of at least 100/300/100 for better protection.
  • Compare quotes from multiple insurers; rates in Mississippi can vary significantly between providers for the same coverage.
  • Ask about available discounts: bundling home and auto, good driver, good student, low mileage, and defensive driving discounts are commonly available.
  • Review your coverage annually, especially after major life changes such as moving, buying a new vehicle, or adding a teen driver.
