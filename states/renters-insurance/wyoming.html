Is Renters Insurance Required in Wyoming?

No state, including Wyoming, legally requires tenants to carry renters insurance. That said, individual landlords and property management companies in Wyoming can — and often do — require proof of a policy as a condition of the lease, particularly for larger apartment complexes. Even where it isn't required, renters insurance is one of the most affordable ways to protect your belongings and cover your liability as a tenant.

Average Renters Insurance Cost in Wyoming

Renters in Wyoming pay an estimated $106 per year ($9/month) for a standard policy, based on 2026 industry average data. Actual premiums vary based on coverage limits, deductible, credit history (where allowed), claims history, and the specific building or neighborhood. Renters who only need modest personal property coverage (around $20,000) typically pay noticeably less than this average, while those insuring higher-value belongings pay more.

What a Standard Policy Covers in Wyoming

A typical renters insurance policy in Wyoming covers three main areas: personal property (your belongings, against fire, theft, and other covered perils), liability (if someone is injured in your rental or you accidentally damage someone else's property), and additional living expenses (temporary housing costs if your rental becomes uninhabitable after a covered loss). Standard policies generally exclude flood and earthquake damage, which require separate coverage.

What Makes Wyoming Renters Insurance Unique

Wyoming is routinely the cheapest or second-cheapest state in the country for renters insurance, thanks to its very low population density and low claim frequency.

Risk Factors Specific to Wyoming

Underwriters in Wyoming weigh a generally low frequency of weather and crime-related claims more heavily than in lower-risk states when pricing renters policies. Reviewing your policy's covered perils against these local risks is worth doing before you assume a standard policy covers everything.

How to Save on Renters Insurance in Wyoming

Because Wyoming renters insurance is already relatively affordable compared to homeowners coverage, small changes can meaningfully cut your premium further.

  • Bundle with auto insurance — most insurers offer a 5–15% discount for combining policies.
  • Raise your deductible from $500 to $1,000 to lower your monthly premium.
  • Install security features like deadbolts, smoke detectors, or a monitored alarm system.
  • Compare quotes from at least 3 insurers annually — rates for identical coverage can vary widely.
  • Choose replacement cost coverage over actual cash value; the premium difference is small but the payout difference is significant.
