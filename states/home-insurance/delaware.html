Home Insurance in Delaware: What You Need to Know

Delaware has relatively low homeowners insurance costs for an East Coast state. The state faces moderate risk from Atlantic hurricanes and nor'easters. Coastal Delaware (Rehoboth Beach, Lewes) faces storm surge risk that requires separate flood insurance. Inland areas have limited severe weather risk compared to most surrounding states.

Average Homeowners Insurance Costs in Delaware

Homeowners in Delaware typically pay approximately $890 per year ($74/month) for homeowners insurance in 2026, based on industry average data for a home with approximately $280,000 in dwelling coverage. Actual premiums vary significantly based on the home's age, construction type, location, claims history, selected coverage limits, and deductible. Homes in high-risk areas — particularly those facing wildfire, hurricane, or flood exposure — may pay significantly more than the state average.

Natural Disaster Risk in Delaware

Primary risks: Hurricane (moderate), Nor'easter (moderate), Flooding (moderate)

Coastal Delaware communities face storm surge and flooding from nor'easters and occasional tropical storms. Sea level rise is increasing flood risk along Delaware Bay. Inland areas are generally low-risk but can experience flooding from heavy rainfall events.

What Makes Delaware Unique

Delaware's small size means that most of the state is within reach of coastal storm systems, but the overall risk profile remains moderate compared to states further south. The growing coastal resort communities on the Delmarva Peninsula have seen increasing flood insurance costs as FEMA updates flood maps.

How to Save on Home Insurance in Delaware

Delaware's competitive insurance market keeps rates moderate. Bundling home and auto is effective. Coastal Delaware homeowners should obtain flood insurance through NFIP or private insurers regardless of whether it is required by their mortgage lender. Home security systems and updated roofing can qualify for meaningful discounts.

  • Compare quotes from at least 3-5 insurers — premiums for identical coverage can vary by hundreds or thousands of dollars annually.
  • Bundle your home and auto insurance with the same carrier for discounts typically ranging from 10-20%.
  • Install a monitored home security system — most insurers offer discounts of 5-15% for qualifying systems.
  • Review your dwelling coverage limit annually to ensure it reflects current construction costs, not just your home's market value.
  • Consider whether you need flood insurance separately — standard homeowners policies do not cover flood damage regardless of cause.

What Standard Homeowners Insurance Covers (and What It Doesn't)

A standard HO-3 homeowners policy in Delaware typically covers your dwelling structure and attached structures against most perils (fire, wind, hail, lightning, vandalism, and theft), your personal property against named perils, liability protection if someone is injured on your property, and additional living expenses if your home is uninhabitable due to a covered loss.

Standard policies in Delaware do not cover flood damage (requires separate NFIP or private flood policy), earthquake damage (requires separate earthquake endorsement or policy), normal wear and tear, or intentional damage. Given Delaware's specific risk profile, homeowners should carefully evaluate whether additional coverage types are warranted.
