Home Insurance in Indiana: What You Need to Know

Indiana is one of the more tornado-prone states in the Midwest, with the southern half of the state having higher frequency. Standard policies cover tornado and hail damage. Flooding along the Wabash and White Rivers can be severe but requires separate flood coverage.

Average Homeowners Insurance Costs in Indiana

Homeowners in Indiana typically pay approximately $1,380 per year ($115/month) for homeowners insurance in 2026, based on industry average data for a home with approximately $220,000 in dwelling coverage. Actual premiums vary significantly based on the home's age, construction type, location, claims history, selected coverage limits, and deductible. Homes in high-risk areas — particularly those facing wildfire, hurricane, or flood exposure — may pay significantly more than the state average.

Natural Disaster Risk in Indiana

Primary risks: Tornado (high), Flooding (moderate), Hail (moderate), Winter storms (moderate)

Southern Indiana has higher tornado frequency than the northern part of the state. Spring hailstorms cause significant roof damage statewide. Lake-effect snow from Lake Michigan affects northwestern Indiana in winter. Flooding along major river systems can be severe in wet years.

What Makes Indiana Unique

Indiana's manufactured and mobile home population is significant — these homes are far more vulnerable to tornado damage than site-built homes. Standard homeowners policies for manufactured homes differ from traditional policies, with different replacement cost provisions and coverage limits that owners should review carefully.

How to Save on Home Insurance in Indiana

Indiana homeowners can reduce premiums by upgrading to impact-resistant roofing, adding storm doors and reinforced garage doors, and installing a monitored security system. Southern Indiana homeowners in tornado-prone areas should verify they have replacement cost coverage rather than actual cash value. Bundling home and auto is consistently effective.

  • Compare quotes from at least 3-5 insurers — premiums for identical coverage can vary by hundreds or thousands of dollars annually.
  • Bundle your home and auto insurance with the same carrier for discounts typically ranging from 10-20%.
  • Install a monitored home security system — most insurers offer discounts of 5-15% for qualifying systems.
  • Review your dwelling coverage limit annually to ensure it reflects current construction costs, not just your home's market value.
  • Consider whether you need flood insurance separately — standard homeowners policies do not cover flood damage regardless of cause.

What Standard Homeowners Insurance Covers (and What It Doesn't)

A standard HO-3 homeowners policy in Indiana typically covers your dwelling structure and attached structures against most perils (fire, wind, hail, lightning, vandalism, and theft), your personal property against named perils, liability protection if someone is injured on your property, and additional living expenses if your home is uninhabitable due to a covered loss.

Standard policies in Indiana do not cover flood damage (requires separate NFIP or private flood policy), earthquake damage (requires separate earthquake endorsement or policy), normal wear and tear, or intentional damage. Given Indiana's specific risk profile, homeowners should carefully evaluate whether additional coverage types are warranted.
