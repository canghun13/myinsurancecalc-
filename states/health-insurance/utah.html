Health Insurance Overview in Utah

Utah expanded Medicaid through a modified approach in 2020, following voter approval of a full expansion in 2018 that the legislature subsequently modified. Utah uses the federal HealthCare.gov marketplace. Utah's young, healthy population and strong employer base mean many residents have employer-sponsored coverage, limiting individual marketplace enrollment.

Marketplace Enrollment in Utah

Utah residents can enroll in individual and family health insurance plans through HealthCare.gov (Federal). Open enrollment runs November 1 – January 15 (federal marketplace). Outside of open enrollment, a qualifying life event (job loss, marriage, birth of a child, move to a new state) triggers a Special Enrollment Period during which you can enroll or change plans.

Medicaid in Utah

Utah Medicaid expanded in 2020, covering adults with incomes up to 138% of the federal poverty level following voter approval and subsequent legislative modifications.

Children's Health Insurance (CHIP) in Utah

CHIP covers children under 19 in families with incomes up to 200% of the federal poverty level.

What Makes Utah Unique

Utah's Medicaid expansion path was contentious — voters approved full expansion in 2018, but the legislature implemented a modified, partial expansion. After legal challenges and negotiations, Utah ultimately implemented a more complete expansion in 2020. The episode highlighted tensions between direct democracy and legislative authority on health policy.

ACA Subsidies: What Utah Residents Should Know

Premium tax credits (subsidies) are available for Utah residents who purchase coverage through the marketplace and have incomes between 100% and 400% of the federal poverty level (or higher in states with additional state subsidies). The subsidy amount is based on the cost of the benchmark Silver plan in your area and your income. For 2026, the enhanced subsidies first enacted in the American Rescue Plan have been extended, providing more substantial assistance across a broader income range than before.

Cost-sharing reductions (CSRs) provide additional savings on deductibles and copays for those who enroll in Silver plans with incomes between 100-250% of the federal poverty level. Silver plans with CSRs often provide Gold or Platinum-level value at Silver premiums, making them the best value for eligible enrollees.

Tips for Choosing Health Insurance in Utah

Utah's young, healthy population often qualifies for favorable marketplace rates. Medicaid covers low-income adults. Utah's strong employer market means many residents have employer coverage. ACA subsidies reduce marketplace costs for eligible residents. Utah's large family sizes mean family coverage amounts should be calculated carefully.

  • Enroll during open enrollment (November 1 – January 15 (federal marketplace)) — missing the deadline means waiting until the next open enrollment unless you have a qualifying life event.
  • Utah has expanded Medicaid, so if your income is at or below 138% of the federal poverty level you likely qualify for Medicaid rather than a marketplace plan — check Medicaid eligibility first before assuming you need to buy marketplace coverage.
  • Don't just compare premiums — also compare deductibles, out-of-pocket maximums, and whether your doctors are in-network.
  • Silver plans with cost-sharing reductions (for incomes 100-250% FPL) typically offer the best overall value despite higher premiums than Bronze plans.
